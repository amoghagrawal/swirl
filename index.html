The Sweet History of Ice Cream

Ice cream has a rich and fascinating history that spans thousands of years. From ancient Chinese frozen treats made with snow and honey to Persian creations with rose water, the journey of ice cream is as flavorful as the dessert itself.

The treat made its way to Europe in the 1500s, with Italian Catherine de Medici introducing it to France. By the 1700s, ice cream recipes were appearing in cookbooks across Europe, and in the early 1800s, Nancy Johnson revolutionized homemade ice cream with her patented hand-cranked freezer.

The ice cream cone as we know it today gained popularity at the 1904 St. Louis World's Fair, and since then, ice cream has become a global phenomenon enjoyed in countless flavors and variations worldwide.
